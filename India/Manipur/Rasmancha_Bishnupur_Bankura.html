Rasmancha Bishnupur Bankura

The Rasmancha is a historical building located at Bishnupur Bankura district West Bengal India. It was commissioned by Mallabhum king Hambir Malla Dev Bir Hambir in 1600 CE. Length and breadth of this temple is 24.5 meter and the height is 12.5 meter. The base or altar of the temple is made of laterite stone and upper part is made with bricks. Upper structure looks like a pyramid. Middle part resembles Bengali huts and the arches of the lower part resembles Islamic architecture.
