Shri Adinath Akhara Bihar

Shri Adinath Akhara Bihar Shri Adinath Akhara is an ashram dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is situated 1.5 km from the Buxar railway station at the confluence of the Ganga and Sone canals. Siddhashrm is a very ancient name of Charitravan. It is mentioned as a pilgrimage in ancient Hindu scriptures including Shrimad Valmiki Ramayana Shiva Purana Vishnu Purana Narada Purana and Agni Purana. Chaurasi Eighty-four Navnath Siddhas spent a term of penance there as authenticated by disc-bearing statues excavated there. Its re-emergence was initiated by Shri Adinath Peethadheeshwar and Shri Trilokinathjee Maharaj Shri Nath Baba jee in 1964. A temple was constructed and in 1967 idols were installed and Yajna was conducted. Shri Adinath Akhara was formally inaugurated in Prayag Kumbh in 1977 by performing Maharudra Yajna. Official Address
