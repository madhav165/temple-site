maa sarala temple Jagatsinghpur

Jhankad is the sanctum sanctorum of Goddess Sarala regarded as one of the most spiritually elevated expressions of Shaktism from time immemorial. Believed as a synthesis of divine figure of Durga and Saraswati the culture of Sarala is an amalgamation of three principal Hindu cults namely Vedic Tantrik and Vaishnavite. It is one of the eight most famous Shakta shrines of Odisha. The main idol carved out of stone is of eight-armed keeping her right feet on the lion in Mahinsha Mardini posture. Constructed by the legendary Bhauma Kings in the 8th Century.The place has a legendary association with sage Parshuram the song of sageYamadagni .Thus this place is of high historical importance as a centre of Parshuram cult Puran describes that parshuram the sixth incarnation of Lord Vishnu engraved the deity by his arrow and worshiped her for wisdom power.It is also associated with the first epic poet of Odisha Adikavi Sarala Das of 15th Century AD.
