Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy Temple Penchalakona

The Deity at this temple is also called Penusila Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy who is Swayambhu i.e. self-manifested. Here two shilas are intertwisted thereby forming a lion head over a man s body. In Telugu Penu means twisted and Sila means stone which means twisted stone. The temple location is historically famous as the place where Sage Kanva used to perform austerities. The temple is located in the Penchalakona valley. Apart from Narasimha Jayanthi the Penchala Swamy festival is popular here which goes on from Vaisakha Shuddha Dwadashi to Bahula Padyami i.e. for a period of 5 days. As in Tirumala devotees offer their hair during this festival. Somasila Narasimha Swamy is another name to the Lord here in this temple. This temple is one of the Nine manifestations of Narasimha Swamy. The Temple Legend Lord Maha Vishnu took the form of Narasimha Swamy Body is of Human and Head is of Lion to kill the demon Hiranyakasyapa. Even after killing the demon the anger was not controlled and he started roaming the forests. To control his anger Goddess Lakshmi took the form of tribal lady Chenchu Lakshmi to please Narasimha Swamy. Chenchu Lakshmi hugged Narasimha Swamy at this place to control the anger. Hug in Telugu called Penu Vesukonuta. Thus the place got the name Penusila as time passes by the name changed to Penchalakona.
