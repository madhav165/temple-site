Choorakkottukavu Devi Temple Thrissur Kerala

Chakkulathukavu Sree Bhagavathy Temple located 30 km southeast of Alappuzha has a rich 3000 year old history associated with it. It is open to people of all faiths and attracts believers from across the State. The Mother Goddess Durga is worshipped here. The famous rivers Pamba and Manimala flow on both sides of the temple. Chakkulathukavu Pongala is the most famous festival associated with the temple. It falls on the Malayalam month of Vrischikam November December and is a grand affair. Pongalani vedayam is a sweet dish that is considered a favourite of the Goddess and women flock in large numbers to the temple to offer the same. Enormous lines are formed outside the temple with devotees carrying this dish in earthen pots for cooking. This dish contains rice porridge molasses and grated coconut.
