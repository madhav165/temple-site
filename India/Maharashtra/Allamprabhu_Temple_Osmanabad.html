Allamprabhu Temple Osmanabad

The Allamprabhu is a unique temple. Situated on a beautiful hill near a magnificent lake the uniqueness of this temple is that both Hindus and Muslims pray in this temple This place is located at 1.5 kilometers 0.93 mi from Bhoom a small town in Osmanabad district Maharashtra. Historically Bhoom was under Nizam of Hyderabad. On September 17 1948 the forces of Nizam of Hyderabad surrendered to the Indian forces. This temple is a nice example of how Hindu and Muslim population lived peacefully in India in the pre-British era Osmanabad is not well connected to other major cities of the country via regular flights. The nearest airport is in Aurangabad at a distance of 198kms. There is no regular trains to Osmanabad from other major cities of the country. The nearest Railway station is at Solapur which is located at a distance of 59kms.
